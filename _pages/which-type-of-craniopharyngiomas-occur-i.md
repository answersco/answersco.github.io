---
title: "Which type of craniopharyngiomas occur in adults? - imaging characteristics"
---
papillary (&quot;Papi&quot; craniopharyngioma)
- solid and no calcifications
- encapsulated
- make sure to note relationship with optic chiasm

Adamantinomatous form
- calcified (not papillary)
- &quot;machinery oil&quot;
- recurs more often
