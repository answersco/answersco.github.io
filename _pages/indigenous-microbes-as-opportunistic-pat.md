---
title: "Indigenous microbes as opportunistic pathogens"
---
occurs when:
1) numbers increase greatly
EX. Candida albicans (fungus formed in yeast form - thrush and vaginal yeast infections)s, Clostridium difficile (usually found in large intestine - ulcerative colitis)
2) gain entrance into sterile areas of the body such as portions urinary tract, portions reproductive tract, lower respiratory tract, blood and other body fluids, &amp; subcutaneous and connective tissues
3) host has failure of immune system (immunocompromised) so microbes are able to gain entrance into sterile areas
