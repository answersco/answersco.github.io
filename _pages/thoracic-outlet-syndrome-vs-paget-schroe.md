---
title: "Thoracic outlet syndrome vs Paget Schroetter"
---
TOS: congenital or acquired compression of the subclavian vessels and brachial plexus as they pass through thoracic inlet
Spectrum: nerve then vein then artery
Compression most commonly by anterior scalene muscle, but could also be from cervical rib, muscular hypertrophy, fibrous bands, paget's, tumors, etc.
Show with arms up and down on angio (occlusion with arms up)

Paget-Schroetter: thoracic outlet syndrome + subclavian vein thrombosis
Usually athletes
Tx: catheter directed lysis and surgical release of offending agent.
