---
title: "Sclerosing cholangitis and DDX for multifocal biliary strictures:"
---
Sclerosing cholangitis: chronic inflammatory and fibrosing process leading to multifocal strictures of the intra- and extrahepatic biliary tree.
More common in men and associated with ulcerative colitis. 
Ultimately leads to biliary cirrhosis and &#8593; risk of cholangiocarcinoma.
Cholangiogram: shows multifocal biliary strictures throughout the intra- and extrahepatic biliary tree. 

DDX for multifocal biliary strictures includes: 
Sclerosing cholangitis
Primary biliary cirrhosis
Multifocal cholangiocarcinoma
Chronic bacterial cholangitis
AIDS cholangitis (usually associated with papillary stenosis).
