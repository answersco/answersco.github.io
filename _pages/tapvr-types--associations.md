---
title: "TAPVR types, associations"
---
TAPVR: cyanotic heart disease with all PV's draining to right heart.
Large PFO (or less commonly ASD) required for survival.
Asplenia: 50% have CHD, nearly 100% TAPVR (85% with additional endocardial cushion defects)

Type 1: Supracardiac- most common
Veins drain above heart- snowman appearance

Type 2: Cardiac- 2nd most common

Type 3: infracardiac- hepatic veins or IVC
May cause pulmonary edema in newborn.
