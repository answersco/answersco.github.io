A philosophical movement which started in Europe in the 1700s and spread to the colonies. It emphasized reason and the scientific method. Writers of this era tended to focus on government, ethics, and science, rather than on religion, imagination, or emotions. Many members of this philosophy rejected traditional religious beliefs in favor of Deism, which holds that the world is run by natural laws without the direct intervention of God.
