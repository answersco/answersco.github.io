---
title: "What are the components of a prioritization matrix table?"
---
Problem, Frequency, Importance, and Feasibility

Prioritization matrix: used to achieve consensus on an issue.
Can rank problems or concepts by specific criteria important to the organization.
After completing the matrix, the team can visualize which problems are most important and prioritize them accordingly.

Create a table with the following components:
Problem: what are the issues/concerns?
Frequency: how often does each issue occur?
Importance: how serious is each issue?
Feasibility: how solvable is each problem?

Each team member ranks each issue&#8594; ratings are totaled&#8594; issues are prioritized
