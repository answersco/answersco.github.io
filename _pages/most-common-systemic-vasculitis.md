---
title: "Most common systemic vasculitis"
---
Giant cell arteritis: usually old men (70-80)
Involves aorta and its major branches, especially those of external carotid (temporal artery).
US of temporal artery: wall thickening
CTA/MRA of axilla: wall thickening, occlusions, dilations, and aneurysms (think crutches in old man)
Elevated ESR and CRP, good response to steroid
Temporal artery biopsy is gold standard.
