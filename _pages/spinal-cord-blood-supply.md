---
title: "Spinal cord blood supply"
---
Anterior spinal artery: arises bilaterally as 2 small branches at the level of the termination of the vertebral arteries. These 2 arteries join around the level of the foramen magnum.

Artery of Adamkiewicz: reinforces anterior spinal artery
75% comes off left side of aorta between T8 and T11
Supplies lower 2/3 of cord
Can get covered by endovascular stent graft

Posterior spinal artery: arises from either the verts or the PICA. Somewhat discontinuous, and reinforced by multiple segmental or radiculopial branches.
