Joubert anomaly (also known as vermian aplasia or molar tooth midbrain-hindbrain malformation) is an autosomal recessive disorder where there is a variable degree of cerebellar vermis agenesis. There is an absence of fibre decussation in the superior cerebellar peduncles and pyramidal tracts
Affected individuals usually present with ataxia and have dysmorphic facies, global developmental delay, hypotonia, rapid breathing and oculomotor apraxia. Typically, patients have nystagmus and inability of smooth pursuit of a moving object
