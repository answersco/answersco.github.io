The upper lobe (A) collapses towards the apex, pulling the horizontal fissure upwards. The middle lobe (B) collapses to form a poorly defined opacity, often obscuring the right heart border. Finally, in lower lobe (C) collapse the oblique fissure is pulled more horizontally and the border of the diaphragm is obscured while the right heart border is generally preserved.
