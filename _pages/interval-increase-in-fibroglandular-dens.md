title: "Interval increase in fibroglandular density:"
---
Interval &#8593; in breast density:
Bilateral &#8593; in fibroglandular density&#8594; usually benign
May be caused by hormonal effects (HRT), breast edema, pregnancy, lactation, and weight loss.
Edema due to systemic causes (e.g. CHF) &#8594; causes bilateral trabecular blurring and skin thickening
Hormone therapy&#8594; can cause &#8593; in fibroglandular density without skin thickening. May also have proliferation of cysts and fibrocystic change.

Unilateral &#8593; in fibroglandular density&#8594; worrisome for lymphatic obstruction, and may be malignant.
