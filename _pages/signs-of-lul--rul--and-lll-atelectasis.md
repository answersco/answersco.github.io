---
title: "Signs of LUL, RUL, and LLL atelectasis:"
---
LUL atelectasis: features the luftsichel sign (air-sickle) &#8594; crescent of air on the frontal XR between the aortic arch and the hyperexpanded superior segment of the LLL.

RUL atelectasis: reverse S sign of Golden seen in RUL collapse due to obstructing mass. 
Juxtaphrenic peak sign: peridiaphragmatic triangular opacity caused by diaphragmatic traction from an inferior accessory fissure or an inferior pulmonary ligament.

LLL atelectasis: heart rotates slightly and the left hilum is pulled down.
Flat waist sign: flattening of the left heart border due to downward shift of hilar structures and cardiac rotation.
