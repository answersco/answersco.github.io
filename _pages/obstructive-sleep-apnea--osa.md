---
title: "Obstructive sleep apnea (OSA)"
---
Pharynx &#8594; Compressed by soft tissues and surrounding structures &#8594; Tone is reduced during sleep &#8594; Upper airway collapse &#8594; Repeated apneic events &#8594; Hypoxia and hypercapnia &#8594; Triggers sympathetic response

-Air flow blocked by anatomical structures (&gt;16in neck circumference in women and &gt;17in for men

-Excess fat around neck

-Tongue, tonsils, or uvula, soft tissue

-Do not get adequate sleep increasing risk for obesity, HTN, MI, and stroke

-At risk for car accidents b/c fall asleep while driving
