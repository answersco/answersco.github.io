---
title: "Typhoid fever"
---
-caused by Salmonella Typhi
-common in USA before 1940
-disease of crowded poor conditions - military troops, city slums
-transmission: fecal oral, direct and someone had typhoid fever and got the bacteria on their hands and touched someone else and ended up digesting the bacteria that way
-carriers exist - small percentage of people will get the disease and recover but are human reservoirs and harbor the bacteria in the digestive tract, usually in the gallbladder and they do not have symptoms
-invasive disease of small intestine and soon gets into the bloodstream instead of staying onto the cell - if left untreated, it can eventually erode into the intestinal wall
-can be fatal
-treatment - antibiotics
