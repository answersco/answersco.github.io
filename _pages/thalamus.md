---
title: "Thalamus"
---
-in diencephalon
-works to correlate several important processes, including consciousness, sleep, and sensory interpretation

-relay center
-where info passes through

all sensory information is routed through the thalamus (visual, auditory, pain, touch, etc.)

the brain's sensory switchboard, located on top of the brainstem; it directs messages to the sensory receiving areas in the cortex and transmits replies to the cerebellum and medulla
