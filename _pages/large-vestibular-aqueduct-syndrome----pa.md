---
title: "Large vestibular aqueduct syndrome: - pathophysiology - most common symptom - unilateral or bilateral? - what is the most common associated other congenital abnormality"
---
- failure of the endolymphatic sac to resorb endolymph, leading to endolymphatic hydrops and dilation

- causes sensorineural hearing loss
- usually bilateral
- associated with absence of the bony modiolus (cochlear deformity - near 100%)
