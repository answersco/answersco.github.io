---
title: "What is Pleomorphic Xanthoastrocytoma? Location, sx, and tx?"
---
PXA is a rare, supratentorial, superficial, low-grade astrocytic tumor often with leptomeningeal involvement. Most often temporal lobe (50%). Often p/w seizure activity in a young adult. Need surgical removal, unresponsive to chemo/RT. Favorable outcome despite aggressive histological appearance.
