---
title: "What is a Penetrating Atheromatus Ulcer (PAU)? - who gets them? main risk factor? - what can they lead to? (2)"
---
an atheromatous plaque that has eroded the inner elastic layer of the aortic wall --&gt; reaches media and produces a hematoma
- people with severe underlying atherosclerosis
- Stanford type B is surgical

Complications:
- lead to Saccular aneursym
- can lead to dissection (&gt; 5 cm)
