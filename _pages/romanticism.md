---
title: "Romanticism"
---
the burgeoning international movement in the arts and literature (in direct response to the Enlightenment and Industrial Revolution) that dominated artistic expression in the first half of the nineteenth century; encompassed poetry, music, painting, history, and literature; glorified nature, emotion, genius, and imagination; challenged the reliance on reason, symmetry, and cool geometric spaces; turned to folklore and medieval legends and common man as hero; sought Nature as a source of spirituality and inspiration; sought out all that was wild, fevered, and disorderly, unique, individual

Major Western artistic style of 1700s and 1800s.Against Neoclassicism, spontaneous, mysterious/ exotic, untamed/ powerful nature, embraces folklore and national traditions, glorification of heroes
