---
title: "Where does clear cell sarcoma of the kidney occur?"
---
Renal medulla

- Most cases demonstrate hemorrhage, necrosis, or cystic change and thus it can be mistaken for multilocular cystic nephroma

- Most often mets to LNs, then bone
(More likely to go to bone than malignant rhabdoid tumor)

- Occurs in 2nd year of life; good prognosis if stage 1
