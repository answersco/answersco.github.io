---
title: "Etiologies of acute upper GI bleeding"
---
Peptic ulcer
Gastritis
Portal HTN (varices)
Mallory-Weiss tear
Marginal ulcer
Iatrogenic (biopsy, gastrostomy)
AVM/angiodysplasia
Dieulafoy lesion (large, tortuous submucosal arteriole in bowel wall that erodes and bleeds)
Tumor
Pseudoaneurysm
Hemobilia
Hemosuccus entericus
Pseudo-GI bleeding (swallowed blood from nasopharyngeal source)
Aortoenteric fistula
