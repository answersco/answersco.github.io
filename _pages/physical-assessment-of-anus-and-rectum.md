---
title: "Physical assessment of anus and rectum"
---
1) lesions, ulcers, fissures (linear break on the margin of the anus), inflammation, and external hemorrhoids 
2) ask the patient to bear down as though having a bowel movement. Assess for the appearance of internal hemorrhoids or fissures and fecal masses.
3) inspect perineal area for skin irritation secondary to diarrhea or fecal incontinence.
