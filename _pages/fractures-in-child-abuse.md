---
title: "Fractures in child abuse:"
---
Highly specific for child abuse:
Classic metaphyseal lesion (metaphyseal corner or bucket handle frx)
Circumferential fracture through the peripheral spongiosa bone of the distal metaphysis, usually of knee or ankle, thought to be due to violent shaking.
Heals quickly (within 10 days) without callus formation
Posterior rib fracture
Scapula fracture
Sternum fracture
Spinous process fracture

Suspicious, but not highly specific:
More than 1 fracture
Fracture out of proportion to history
Digital fracture in infants
Long bone fracture in non-ambulatory child
Complex skull fracture
