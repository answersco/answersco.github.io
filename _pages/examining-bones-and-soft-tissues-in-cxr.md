This is primarily to look for fractures, lytic lesions, or sclerosis suggesting metastases. While you are looking at the ribs, the costophrenic angles&#8212;formed at the intersection of the lateral ends of the hemidiaphragms with the rib cage&#8212;should be assessed. Blunting of these might be caused by small pleural effusions.
