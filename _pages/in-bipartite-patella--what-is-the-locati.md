---
title: "In bipartite patella, what is the location of the accessory ossicle, and what are the most common symptoms and MRI findings?"
---
Bipartite patella: may be painful, depending on the degree of abnormal motion at the synchondrosis.
The accessory ossicle is usually in the superolateral patella, and is frequently bilateral.T
They most commonly preesent with anterior knee pain.
Most common MRI finding is bone edema, hyperintense signal on T2-FS in the bipartite fragment.
