---
title: "What is the CXR appearance of atypical mycoplasma pneumonia."
---
Common: bilateral perbronchial and interstitial infiltrates, patchy opacities and alveolar consolidations that tend to predominate in the lower lobes
Characteristic but less common: interstitial, reticulonodular opacity of a single lobe
Very common: Hilar LAD
Less common: pleural effusions, empyema, cavitation
