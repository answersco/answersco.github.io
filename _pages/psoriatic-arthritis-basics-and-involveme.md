---
title: "Psoriatic arthritis basics and involvement of the hands:"
---
Psoriatic arthritis &#8594; most commonly affects the hands
Unlike RA, mineralization is preserved
Sacroiliitis, if present, is usually asymmetric
Hands&#8594; XR hallmark is diffuse soft-tissue swelling of an entire digit&#8594; sausage digit.
Pencil-in-cup erosions&#8594; most commonly affect DIPs
Hand findings usually bilateral, but involvement tends to be asymmetric
Severe arthritis mutilans variant&#8594; can cause marked deformity and telescoping digits
May have fluffy periostitis and ill-defined erosions of the joint margins.
