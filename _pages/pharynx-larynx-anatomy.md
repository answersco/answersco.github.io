---
title: "Pharynx/larynx anatomy"
---
Nasopharynx: base of skull to soft palate
Orophaynx: hard palate to hyoid bone
Hypopharynx/laryngopharynx: hyoid bone (tip of epiglottis) to cricoid cartilage


Important anatomic structures:
1. Cricopharyngeus: the true upper esophageal sphincter, also represents border between the pharynx and cervical esophagus 
2. Note: when you swallow the larynx does two things 1: moves up 2: move anterior
