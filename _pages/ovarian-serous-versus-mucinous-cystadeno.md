---
title: "Ovarian serous versus mucinous cystadenocarcinoma"
---
Serous cystadenocarcinoma: frequently bilateral, and appear as mixed solid and cystic masses
Solid portions demonstrate avid enhancement.
Often concomitant ascites.
Mucinous cystadenocarcinomas: typically unilateral, large, and in older patients than serous tumors.
Typically present as a multiloculated cystic mass containing mucin-rich T1-hyperintense fluid.
