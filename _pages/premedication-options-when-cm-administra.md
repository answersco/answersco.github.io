---
title: "Premedication options when CM administration is required in a shorter time-frame:"
---
In decreasing order of desirability...
I: Methylprednisolone sodium succinate (Solu-Medrol) 40 mg or hydrocortisone sodium succinate (Solu-Cortef) 200 mg IV q4h until study plus diphenhydramine 50 mg IV 1 hour prior to injection.
II: Dexamethasone sodium sulfate (Decadron) 7.5 mg or betamethasone 6.0 mg IV q4h until study in patient with known allergy to methylprednisolone, ASA, or NSAIDS, especially if asthmatic. Also add diphenhydramine 50 mg IV 1 hour prior to study.
III: omit steroids entirely and give diphenhydramine 50 mg IV (e.g patients with DM, uncontrolled HTN, TB, systemic fungal infections, PUD, or diverticulitis).
