---
title: "Perc chole using transperitoneal approach: advantages and disadvantages"
---
Advantages:
-Direct approach, avoiding the necessity of going through the liver.
-If perc cholecystolithotomy (PCCL) is being considered, a transperitoneal approach allows safe, easy dilatation of a percutaneous track.

Disadvantages:
-Gallbladder is more mobile near the fundus and may be more difficult to puncture.
-Transverse colon may overlie fundus of GB.
-Seldinger technique prone to guidewire buckling when a transperitoneal approach is used.
