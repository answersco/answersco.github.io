---
title: "Spinal cord syndromes"
---
Central cord: old lady with spondylosis or young person with bad extension injury.
-Upper extremity deficit worse than lower (corticospinal tracts are lateral in LE)

Anterior cord: flexion injury
-immediate paralysis

Brown-Sequard: rotation injury or penetrating trauma
-1/2 motor, other 1/2 sensory

Posterior cord: uncommon, sometimes with hyperextension
-Loss of proprioception and temperature sensation.
