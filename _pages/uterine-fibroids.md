---
title: "Uterine fibroids"
---
Can be in three locations: 1) subserosal, 2) submucosal, 3) intramual (MOST COMMON)
Appearance: Typically hypoechoid on ultrasound, MRI: T1/T2 DARK w/ variable enhancement, fibroids with HIGHER t2 signal are said to respond BETTER to IR emboliaztion. They CAN contrain fat, variant called lipoleiomyoma, CT: peripheal popcorn calcifications
*<b>REMEMBER</b>* fibroids in general enhance AVIDLY
