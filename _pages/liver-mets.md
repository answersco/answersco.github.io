---
title: "Liver Mets"
---
hypo, hyper, cystic

- 1st ALWAYS think colon (think portal venous flow) 
- Calcified mets are usually related to a mucinous neoplasm (colon, ovary, pancreas)

Important ultrasound points:
- HYPERechoic mets are usually HYPERvascular (renal, melanoma, carcinoid, choriocarcinoma, thyroid, islet cell)
- HYPOechoic mets are usually HYPOvascular (colon, lung, pancreas)
