---
title: "Physiologic periosteal reaction and other causes of periostitis in a newborn"
---
&quot;Physiologic periostitis of the newborn&quot; - not actually in newborns. Seen around 3 months of age, and should resolve by 6 months.
Proximal involvement (femur) occurs before distal (tibial). 
Always involves the diaphysis.

Other causes:
Neuroblastoma mets
Child abuse
