---
title: "Other pediatric liver mass with elevated AFP:"
---
HCC&#8594; usually in background of cirrhosis
Cirrhosis in child&#8594; could be from alpha-1-antitrypsin deficiency, glycogen storage disease, tyrosinemia, biliary atresia, and chronic viral hepatitis.
AFP is elevated (similar to hepatoblastoma)
Imaging similar to adult HCC, as heterogeneous hepatic mass with early arterial enhancement and rapid washout. Common venous invasion.
