---
title: "Standard precautions"
---
-also called universal 
-decrease transmission from blood borne pathogens regardless of diagnosis
-wear gloves when handling feces, urine, nasal secretions, vomitus &amp; sputum
-implemented with every patient

-wash hands, wear mask, wear gloves, wear gown
-developed as an outgrowth of universal precautions
-established to expand the focus of universal precautions
-primary method to prevent transmission of infectious agents from both recognized and unrecognized sources to HCP, pt's, and facility visitors
-new additions include respiratory hygiene and safe injection practices
