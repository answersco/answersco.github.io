---
title: "Typical findings in hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy (HIE)"
---
Severe HIE typically affects the gray matter, including the cerebral cortex, hippocampi, and basal ganglia&#8594; similar distribution to that seen with severe hypoglycemia.
MRI&#8594; FLAIR and/or DWI hyperintensity of affected regions.
CT&#8594; loss of gray-white differentiation, diffuse cerebral hypoattenuation, and sulcal effacement.
White cerebellum sign&#8594; typical sparing of cerebellum, which appears bright compared to affected supratentorial brain.
