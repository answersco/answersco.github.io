---
title: "Typical adult dosing and reversal of heparin during angio"
---
Rarely necessary in diagnostic peripheral angio unless the catheter impedes flow in a diseased or small vessel
Typical adult dose: 3000-5000U IV bolus, followed by 1000U each hour.
Monitored via activated clotting time (ACT) with target of &gt; 250 seconds

Reversed with slow IV bolus of protamine, usually 10 mg Protamine for every 1000U of unfractionated heparin
-Does not reverse LMWH
