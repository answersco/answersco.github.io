---
title: "Where do you most commonly see an aberrant carotid artery?"
---
Represents an enlarged inferior tympanic artery (from maxillary or superficial temporal ECA) anastomosed with an enlarged caroticotympatnic artery

* Runs through the tympanic cavity and joints up with the horizontal carotid canal

* The vertical segment of carotid canal is ABSENT OR HYPOPLASTIC and the Cervical ICA is UNDERDEVELOPED

** MUST REVIEW RP 46a 5/18!
