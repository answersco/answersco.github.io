---
title: "Most common bronchial tumor in children?"
---
Carcinoid. Much less common in adults.

Carcinoid: spectrum of neuroendocrine neoplasms including low-grade carcinoid, aggressive carcinoid, and SCLC
Most common bronchial tumor in children, though rare in adults
Almost always distal to carina
Rarely secrete hormones such as ACTH (on spectrum including SCLC)
CT&#8594; endoluminal bronchial mass with homogeneous arterial enhancement

Other enhancing endobronchial masses include mucoepidermoid carcinoma, hemangioma, and glomus tumor

Mucoepidermoid carcinoma
Younger patients than ACC
CT: oval or round endobronchial mass, indistinguishable from carcinoid.
