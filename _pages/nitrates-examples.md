---
title: "Nitrates examples"
---
1) nitroglycerin
-given IV, sublingual, or topical
2) amyl nitrate
-given inhalation
3) isosorbide mononitrate
given PO
4) isosorbide dinitrate
-given PO and sublingual
-have a substantial first-pass effect meaning the effect of the medication is decreased significantly from GI tract
-PO forms of medication are formulated specifically and given in specific doses with high first-pass effect in mind
-usually seen IV, sublingual, and topical to get highest amount into bloodstream while bypassing the first-pass effect
-decreases BP, decreases left ventricular workload, decreases O2 consumption, relaxes muscular smooth muscle, decreases venous return
