---
title: "How does a placental site trophoblastic tumor present?"
---
The history of persistent modest elevation of B-hCG following a normal pregnancy and the presence of a mass centered in the subendometrial myometrium

- impossible to distinguish from choriocarcinoma 

- rarest form of gestational trophoblastic disease. It represents a neoplastic proliferation of intermediate trophoblasts that invade the myometrium at the placental site after pregnancy
