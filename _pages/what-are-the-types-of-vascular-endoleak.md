---
title: "What are the types of vascular endoleak?"
---
Type I (Graft-Related) Inadequate seal at proximal or distal landing zones of graft. 1A: Proximal. 1B: Distal. 1C: Iliac occluder (aorto-uni-iliac EVAR).
Type II (Nongraft) Collateral blood flow into aneurysm sac retrograde from lumbar, IMA, or other
2A: Single vessel (simple); 2B: Two or more
Type III Device Failure
3A: Junctional separation of modular devices
3B: Endograft fracture or holes
Type IV Graft fabric porosity (rarely seen now)
Type V Endotension, aneurysm expansion in absence of leak.
