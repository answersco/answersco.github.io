---
T2 and MRCP - low signal
High central protein may give them T1 hyperintensity but in general cholesterol stones T1 hypo and pigmented T1 hyper

mobile echogenic with acoustic shadows

THINK sickle cell if you see gall stones in a pediatric patient

-excess cholesterol precipitates out of blood
-blocks drainage
-treated by lithotripsy (ultrasound), cholecystectomy (surgical removal)
