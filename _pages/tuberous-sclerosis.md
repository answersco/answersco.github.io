---
title: "Tuberous sclerosis:"
---
Tuberous sclerosis: +/- classical triad of adenoma sebaceum, seizures, and MR
MRI: multiple T2-hyperintense white matter cortical or subcortical tubers (hamartomas) and subependymal nodules
15% develop subependymal giant cell astrocytoma (SEGA) &#8594; may appear on imaging as a newly-enhancing or enlarging subependymal nodule
Extracranial manifestations: 
Renal: multiple AMLs
Cardiac: rhabdomyoma
Lung: lymphangioleiomyomatosis

Skin lesions: Adenoma sebaceum. Ash-leaf spots. Brain lesions: Subependymal hamartomas. Cortical tubers. Subependymal giant cell astrocytomas located at foramina of Monro (May lead to hydrocephalus)

SE Ca nodules, SEGA, cort tubers, white matter lesions, large vents

Autosomal dominant
Hamartomas everywhere
Lung: LAM
Cardiac: Rhabdomyoma/sarcoma
Brain: Cortical and subcortical tubers, subependymal nodules, SEGA
Renal: AML, RCC at a younger age, cysts
