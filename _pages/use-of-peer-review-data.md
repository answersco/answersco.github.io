Use of peer review data:

Peer review data are collected confidentially by a central authority (such as the departmental quality committee)

Peer review data are immune to legal action or discovery &#8594; constitutes a &quot;safe&quot; form of self-regulation among qualified radiologists.
Data are fully protected from medicolegal discovery and enjoy a privileged status with regard to malpractice law.

Peer review data are often required by accreditation bodies or regulatory bodies to ensure ongoing QA processes are in place.

Data do not contribute to public reporting, such as the CMS &quot;Hospital Compare&quot; website

Peer review is often linked to OPPE and FPPE
