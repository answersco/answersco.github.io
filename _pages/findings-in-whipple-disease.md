---
title: "Findings in Whipple disease:"
---
Whipple disease: due to infection by Tropheryma whippelii &#8594; manifests as malabsorption and abdominal pain, possibly with arthralgias and &#8593; skin pigmentation
Causes low-attenuation adenopathy&#8594; may appear similar to cavitating mesenteric lymph node syndrome seen in celiac disease
XR: thickening and nodularity of duodenal and proximal SB folds
Unlike celiac disease, there is no hypersecretion.
