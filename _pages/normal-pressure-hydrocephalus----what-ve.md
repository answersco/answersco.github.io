title: "Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus: - what ventricles are most affected - symptom buzz-phrase - MRI findings - treatment"
---
- frontal and temporal horns of lateral ventricles most affected
- &quot;wet, wacky, and wobbly&quot; (urinary incontinence, confusion, ataxia)
- MRI: transependymal flow of CSF and/or flow void in aqueduct and 3rd ventricle
- Tx: surgical shunting
