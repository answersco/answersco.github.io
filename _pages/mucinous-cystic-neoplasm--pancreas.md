---
title: "Mucinous cystic neoplasm (pancreas)"
---
Mucinous neoplasm is the MOTHER lesion 
Almost always found in women and is considered to be premalignant, i.e., they MUST come out
Location: found in the BODY and TAIL of the pancreas
Note: This is NOT the same thing as an IPMN, i.e., these do NOT communicate with the duct
They are typically UNILOCULAR, usually larger than 2 cm
