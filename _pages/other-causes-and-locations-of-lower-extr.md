---
title: "Other causes and locations of lower extremity ulcers"
---
Severe diabetic neuropathy: can develop ulceration over pressure points such as the metatarsal head. 
-Usually painless and associated with callus formation.

Venous ulcers: distinguished from lesions of arterial insufficiency by their typical medial location around the medial ankles in the lower leg, rich vascularity, and associated manifestations of chronic venous stasis.
