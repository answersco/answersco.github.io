---
title: "What lesions commonly occur in the posterior half of the lateral ventricle?"
---
Lesions that occur in the posterior half of the lateral ventricle:
Choroid plexus papilloma&#8594; may have vascular stalk or pedicle
Choroid plexus carcinoma&#8594; large, ugly lesion in patient &lt; 5 years
Intraventricular meningioma&#8594; older female (30-60)
Choroid plexus xanthogranuloma&#8594; &lt; 1 cm, potentially with restricted diffusion

Subependymal giant cell astrocytoma: found in anterior half of the lateral ventricle near Foramen of Monro, and is associated with tuberous sclerosis
