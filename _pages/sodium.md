title: "Sodium"
---
-90% in ECF
-most abundant cation
-major contributor to maintaining water balance
-get most of our sodium through diet and drinks as well
-normal serum value: 135-145 mEq/L

135-145 mmol/L
-most abundant positive electrolyte in extracellular fluid
-plays a large role in fluid volume and osmolarity of the blood 
-if pt has low sodium it is called hyponatremia, can be related to heart or kidney failure, can cause confusion, weakness
-and if it is high it is hypernatremia which is usually due to dehydration or not enough fluid so the sodium is more concentrated, can have orthostatic hypotension, dizziness, thirsty
-both hypo-/hypernatremia cause mental status changes
