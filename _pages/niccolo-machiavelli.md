---
He wrote &quot;The Prince&quot;, dedicated to Lorenzo de Medici, which contained a secular analysis of ruling a state; he analyzed political power and effective methods to keep it, observing that &quot;it is better to be feared than loved: and the &quot;End justifies the means&quot;; he also wrote other works that analyzed types of governments and their typical rise and fall.

(1469-1527) diplomat and political writer most famous for The Prince, a work instructing rulers on how to govern
