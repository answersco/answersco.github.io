---
title: "Raphael"
---
1483-1520 Short but productive life. Worked in Florence and Rome. Well-known for painting Madonnas, humanized portrayals of the Virgin Mary with the baby Jesus. Painted frescoes in Vatican Palace - espec. The School of Athens &amp; The Triumph of Religion - reflect artist's strong interest in classical antiquity and Christian religion.

Renaissance painter most famous for his &quot;School of Athens&quot; fresco reflecting classicism in his admiration of ancient scholars; the painting also incorporated portraits of Leonardo, Michelangelo and Raphael himself which reinforced individualism
