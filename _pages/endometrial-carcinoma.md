---
title: "Endometrial carcinoma:"
---
Endometrial carcinoma: most common female gynecologic malignancy
Caused by prolonged estrogen exposure: specific risk factors include nulliparity, HRT, and Tamoxifen therapy
Presence and extent of myometrial invasion is key for staging
Premenopausal patient: an intact junctional zone confirms that there is no myometrial invasion.
Postmenopausal patients: junctional zone cannot be distinguished
Depth of myometrial invasion correlates with the presence of LN metastases
Post-contrast images demonstrate the tumor with the highest conspicuity&#8594; endometrial cancer enhances less avidly than the surrounding myometrium.
