---
title: "Serous cystadenoma (pancreas)"
---
This is going to be the grandma lesion (think, grandmas are serious)
Appearance: MULTIPLE, SMALL cysts - also remmeber that grandmas are SMALL. Also think CENTRAL calfications
Location: Most commonly located in the pancreatic head - This is opposed to mucinous which is almsot always in the pancreatic body or tail
These do NOT communicate with the pancreatic duct (contrary to IPMN's that do communicate with the duct)
CLASSIC: 20% of the time will have a central scar 

Associations: VHL
