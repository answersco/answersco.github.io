---
title: "Pancreatic endocrine tumors: basics"
---
Pancreatic endocrine neoplasms: tend to be hypervascular and are best seen in late arterial phase. Most are solid unless large.
Hyperfunctioning tumors&#8594; present due to symptoms of endocrine excess
Non-hyperfunctioning tumors: tend to be larger at diagnosis
May undergo cystic change&#8594; should be considered in DDX of a cystic pancreatic neoplasm
These may also have central necrosis and calcification when large
Hypervascular liver mass with associated pancreatic mass is likely a metastatic pancreatic endocrine neoplasm.
