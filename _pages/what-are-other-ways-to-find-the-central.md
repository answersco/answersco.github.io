---
title: "What are other ways to find the central sulcus? (6)"
---
-Superior Frontal sulcus/pre-central sulcus sign:
--- posterior end of the superior frontal sulcus joins the pre-central sulcus

- Inverted omega (sigmoid hook): corresponds to motor band

- Bifid posterior central sulcus: posterior central sulcus has a bifid appearance (85%)

- Thin post-central gyrus sign: precentral gyrus is thicker

- Intersection: the intraparietal sulcus intersects the post central sulcus (almost always works)

- Midline sulcus sign: the most prominent sulcus that reaches midline is the central sulcus
