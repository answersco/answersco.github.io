---
title: "SVC syndrome"
---
due to lung cancer compressing and obstructing the SVC --&gt; swelling/plethora of the head and neck and distension of venous collaterals. Usually Bronchogenic carcinoma - small cell. oother causes - fibrosing mediastinitis and indwelling catheters

edema of face, neck, upper torso with formation of collateral veins on upper chest. usually from bronchogenic carcinoma. may be from intravascular devices.
