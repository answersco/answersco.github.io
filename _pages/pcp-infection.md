---
title: "PCP infection:"
---
Pneumocystis jiroveci: may cause PNA in patient with CD4 &lt; 200 cells/cc
CXR: may be normal.
Classic finding: bilateral perihilar (central) airspace opacities with peripheral sparing.
CT: classic appearance is geometric perihilar GGO, sometimes with crazy paving (GGO and interlobular septal thickening).
Normal CT rules-out PCP.
Propensity to cause upper lobe pneumatoceles, which may predispose to PTX or pneumomediastinum.
