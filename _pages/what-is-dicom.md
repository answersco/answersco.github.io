---
title: "What is DICOM?"
---
Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine. An image based medical protocol that specifies image formats

DICOM (Digital Imaging and Communications in Medicine) is the standard for handling, storing, printing, and transmitting information in medical imaging. DICOM standards are set by the American College of Radiology (ACR) and NEMA (National Electrical Manufacturers Association). 

HL7 is a standard for transfer of clinical and administrative data between hospital information systems. HTML is standard mark-up language used to create web pages. TCP/IP is an internet communication protocol. JPEG is a lossy image compression format.
