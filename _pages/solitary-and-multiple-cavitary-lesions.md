---
title: "Solitary and multiple cavitary lesions:"
---
Solitary cavitary lesion: maximum wall thickness of &lt; 4 mm usually benign, &gt; 15 mm usually malignant
Primary bronchogenic carcinoma: squamous cell and adenocarcinoma are known to cavitate, while small-cell virtually never cavitates
TB: upper lobe cavitation

Multiple cavitary lesions: typically vascular 
Septic emboli
Vasculitis: Wegener granulomatosis is especially prone to cavitation
Metastases: SCC and uterine caricnosarcoma
