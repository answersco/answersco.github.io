---
title: "Swyer-James"
---
Classic unilateral hyperlucent lung with diminished # and caliber of vessels.
Typically after viral lung infection, resulting in post-infectious obliterative bronchiolitis.
Size of the affected lobe is smaller than normal lobe (not hyperexpanded).

DDX includes aspirated FB, unilateral absence of PA, and Poland syndrome (absent pectoralis muscle, often with limb deformities).

Unilateral lucent lung
The lucent lung is NOT hyperexpanded (it's normal size)
This normally occurs after an infection in a young kid and causes obliterative bronchiolitis

Happens after a viral infection
