---
title: "Periventricular leukomalacia"
---
Infarction of periventricular tissue
Initially increased echogenicity which turns cystic

Ischemic/hemorrhagic insult in utero causes leukomalacia within a watershed distribution dorsal and lateral to the lateral ventricles 
Large proportion of these cases go on to develop cerebral palsy
The first atrophy is very hard to see however it will develop into cystic change in about 3 weeks
This is more common in premature infants less than 1500g
