---
title: "Meconium aspiration"
---
newborn

Intrauterine fetal distress can lead to passage of meconium. Aspirated meconium particles cause obstruction of small peripheral bronchioles. Results in areas of subsegmental atelectasis and areas of overdistension. Coarse reticulonodular or nodular appearance of lungs.

Not TTN or pna bc known clinically
Usually somewhat asymmetric 
Usually ETT

Findings: Hyperinflation + streaky opacities (probs from downstream atelectasis)
Demographic: Post-term babies and term 
Patho: All secondary to chemical aspiration 
Associations: Pneumothorax in 20-40%
