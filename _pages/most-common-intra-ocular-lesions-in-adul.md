---
title: "Most common intra-ocular lesions in adult and child"
---
Melanoma: most common intra-ocular lesion in an adult &#8594; enhancing soft-tissue lesion in the back of an adult eye

Retinoblastoma: usually before age 3 (rare after 6 years)
Calcification
T2 bright (Coat's disease and PHPV are T2-dark)
Trilateral: both eyes + pineal gland
Quadrilateral: both eyes + pineal + suprasellar
