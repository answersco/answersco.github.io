---
title: "Post-infectious causes of periosteal reaction in a newborn"
---
Congenital rubella: bony changes in 50%, usually in first few weeks of life.
Classic &quot;celery stalk&quot; appearance from generalized lucency of the metaphysis.

Syphilis: bony changes in 95%, but do NOT occur until 6-8 weeks of life.
Metaphyseal lucent bands and periosteal reaction along the long bones often seen.
Buzzword: &quot;Wimberger sign&quot; or destruction of the medial portion of the proximal tibial metaphysis.
