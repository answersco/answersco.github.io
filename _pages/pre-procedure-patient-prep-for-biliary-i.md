---
title: "Pre-procedure patient prep for biliary intervention"
---
Antibiotic prophylaxis is mandatory
-Common regimens include zosyn (piperacillin/tazobactam) 4.5 g IV, gentamicin 80 mg IV, and ampicillin 1 g IV.
-Zosyn is broad-spectrum with activity against gram-+, gram-neg, and aerobic infections, with high levels in bile.
-For biliary drainage, it is common to continue treatment for 2 days tid with zosyn.

In jaundiced patients with malignant obstruction, IV fluids are often started before procedure, and continued for 3-4 days, commonly 2.5 L of Hartman's solution daily.
