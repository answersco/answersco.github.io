---
title: "What makes a screening test &quot;Very high utility?&quot;"
---
Screening tests are very good if: (1) there is a sufficiently high prevalence of the disease, (2) the disease causes significant morbidity and mortality, (3) there is available, effective, and acceptable treatment, (4) there is a benefit to detecting the disease before symptoms develop, (5) outcomes improve with early treatment, (6) the tests are characterized by good sensitivity, good specificity, low cost, and low risk, (7) confirmatory tests are available, practical, and accurate.
