---
title: "Infection in AIDS by CT pattern (focal or multifocal airspace opacity, GGO)"
---
Focal airspace opacity:
Bacterial infection (strep PNA) most common.
TB if low CD4
Chronic: lymphoma or Kaposi sarcoma (flame-shaped perihilar)

Multi-focal airspace opacity: bacterial or fungal infection

GGO: PCP (bilateral perihilar, sparing lung periphery, +/- thin-walled cysts, PTX)
CMV if CD4 &lt; 100
