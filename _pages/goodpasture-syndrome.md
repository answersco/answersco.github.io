---
title: "Goodpasture syndrome"
---
Autoimmune pulmonary-renal syndrome, usually in young men.
Very nonspecific appearance: bilateral coalescent airspace opacities that look a lot like edema (but represent hemorrhage).
Resolve quickly (within 2 weeks)
Recurrent bleeding can lead to fibrosis
Pulmonary hemosiderosis: iron deposition manifesting as small, ill-defined nodules.

Damage to alveolar and renal glomerular basement membranes by cytotoxic antibody.
