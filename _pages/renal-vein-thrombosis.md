---
title: "Renal vein thrombosis"
---
Causes include dehydration, indwelling UVC (most common in neonates), and nephrotic syndrome (most common in adults).
Presents with flank pain, enlarged kidney, delayed nephrogram.
Doppler: will show reversed arterial diastolic flow and absent venous flow

In setting of trauma, may show as persistent nephrogram in renal vein injury/thrombosis.

Enlarged echogenic kidney with loss of corticomedullary differentiation

Nephrotic syndrome
Protein C or S deficiency
Dehydration
Polythycemia
Burns
Adrenal hemorrhage
Transplants
