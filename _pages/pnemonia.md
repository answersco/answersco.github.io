---
title: "Pnemonia"
---
Presence of bacterial, mycobacterial, viral, fungal, or foreign substance in the lung &#8594; causes inflammation in lung tissues (alveoli) &#8594; affects ventilation and diffusion &#8594; decrease in alveolar oxygen tension mismatched ventilation / perfusion &#8594; arterial hypoxemia

-Inflammation of the lung parenchyma caused by microorganisms (pneumonitis) 

-One of the most common causes of death in the United States
Number of deaths: 51,811 

Types:
1) Community acquired pneumonia (CAP):
-got it outside of the hospital
2) Health care associated pneumonia (HCAP):
-got it from nursing home
3) Hospital-acquired pneumonia (HAP):
-got it during stay in the hospital 
-develop symptoms after at least 48 hours
4) Ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP):
-been on ventilator for more than 48 hours
-very big in ICU's for trying to prevent 
5) Pneumonia in the immunocompromised host 
-cancer, HIV/AIDS, autoimmune diseases
6) Aspiration pneumonia:
-could have occurred during seizure, stroke, vomiting, choking on food/drink
