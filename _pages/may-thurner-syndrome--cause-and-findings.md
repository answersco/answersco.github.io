---
title: "May-Thurner syndrome: cause and findings"
---
May-Thurner syndrome: may present with chronic symptoms of left leg edema and heaviness, or acute thrombosis.
-Due to compression of the left CIV between the right CIA and the lumbosacral spine.
-Patients shouldn't be treated unless appropriate symptoms are present. Seen in up to 25% of asymptomatic adult population.

3 venographic stages have been described:
-Asymptomatic compression of the left CIV with no filling of collaterals and pressure gradient &lt; 2 mmHg
-Intraluminal webs or spurs
-Thrombosis
