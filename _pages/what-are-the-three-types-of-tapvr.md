---
title: "What are the three types of TAPVR?"
---
Supracardiac (type 1): &quot;Vertical&quot; common pulmonary vein carries blood from both lungs and joins left innominate vein
Intracardiac/cardiac (type 2): Common pulmonary vein joins coronary sinus or right atrium directly.
Infracardiac (type 3): Common pulmonary vein traverses diaphragm to join portal vein, ductus venosus or IVC. Often obstructed leading to pulmonary congestion. Small Heart. Cyanotic at birth.
ALL ARE EXTRACARDIAC LEFT TO RIGHT SHUNTS AND MUST HAVE ASD OR VSD.
