---
title: "What vasculitis is associated with hepatitis B?"
---
PAN (medium-vessel): this and Buerger's are the only 2 that are more common in men.
Renal (90%), cardiac (70%), and GI (50-70%) most common
Microaneurysm formation, primarily at branch points, followed by infarction.
Will show kidneys with microaneurysms or wedge-shaped infarcts.
Associated with hepatitis B.
Similar findings in kidneys with meth abuse.
