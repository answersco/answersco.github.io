---
title: "Osteomalacia"
---
Diffuse bone demineralization that is usually secondary to a problem with vitamin D metabolism (think liver or kidneys)
Look for perpindicular areas of lucency to the cortex at the femoral necks and the superior pubic rami (these are a type of INSUFFICIENCY FRACTURE)
These are seen commonly in osteomalacia and rickets

a disease of adults that is characterized by softening of the bones and is analogous to rickets in the young

no vit d. in adults - renal osteodystrophy, (pseudo)hyperparathyroidism with resportion of clavicle, osteopenia. In children, rickets. May be indistinguishable from osteoporosis, except looser's zones and pseudofractures.
