---
title: "Methodism"
---
A Protestant Christian variety founded by John Wesley in the 18th C that insisted strict self-discipline and a methodical approach to religious study and observance; it emphasized an intense personal salvation and a life of thrift, abstinence, and hard work; this movement taught that all men and women who sought salvation might be saved, giving the people a message of hope and was characterized by concern with social welfare and public morals
