---
title: "IR evaluation of occult functioning islet-cell tumors (insulinoma and gastrinoma)"
---
These can be localized by sampling the hepatic veins following selective arterial injections of a secretagogue.
Rise in the hepatic vein concentration of gastrin and insulin helps localize angiographically occult lesions.

This procedures can also be performed for evaluation of pancreatic nesidioblastosis (pancreatic beta cell hypertophy without focal mass), which occurs as rare complication of RNY gastric bypass.
