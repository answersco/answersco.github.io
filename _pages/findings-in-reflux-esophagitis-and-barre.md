---
title: "Findings in reflux esophagitis and Barrett esophagus:"
---
Reflux esophagitis: appears as thickened distal esophageal folds
Chronic esophagitis and scarring after prolonged exposure: causes a smoothly-tapered stricture above the GEJ

Barrett esophagus: imaging demonstrates a featureless distal esophagus, with signs of active reflux esophagitis (mucosal granularity and superficial erosions) more proximally.
Often associated with esophageal stricture&#8594; abnormally high in location compared to a peptic stricture
