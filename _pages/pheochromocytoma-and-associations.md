---
title: "Pheochromocytoma and associations"
---
Pheochromocytoma: usually large at presentation (&gt; 3 cm)
Heterogeneous on CT
T2 bright
MIBG &gt; Octreotide due to octreotide uptake in kidneys
10% extra-adrenal&#8594; organ of Zuckerkandl (usually at IMA)
10% bilateral, 10% children, 10% hereditary, 10% not active (no HTN)

Syndromes:
VHL &gt; MEN IIa and IIb
Also NF-1, Sturge-Weber, and TS
Carney triad: extra-adrenal pheo, GIST, and pulmonary chondroma (hamartoma)
