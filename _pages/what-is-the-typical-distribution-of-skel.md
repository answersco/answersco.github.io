---
title: "What is the typical distribution of skeletal lesions in Ollier disease?"
---
Answer: predominantly unilateral or asymmetric
-Nonhereditary dysplasia with multiple enchondromas.
-Lesions commonly involve the metaphyseal regions of long bones. Skull and spine are spared.
-Lesions regress or stabilize after skeletal maturation.
-MRI includes lobular margins and internal T2 hyperintensity.
