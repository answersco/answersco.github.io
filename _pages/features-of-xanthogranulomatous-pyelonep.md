---
title: "Features of xanthogranulomatous pyelonephritis?"
---
Terminology
Chronic infection of kidney and surrounding tissues characterized by destruction of renal parenchyma and replacement by lipid-laden macrophages
Imaging
Best diagnostic clue: Obstructing calculus, atrophic or nonfunctioning kidney, perirenal fibrofatty proliferation
Diffuse or focally absent nephrogram
Centrally obstructing calculus; staghorn (75%)

Middle-aged females with UTIs. Focal hypofunctioning renal mass. Infection-based stones.

Female predominance. History of UTIs. Nephrolithiasis. Renal enlargement. Renal hypofunction. Fractured calculus. Renal cysts. Extrarenal extension common.
