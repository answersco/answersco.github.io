---
title: "TIPS versus BRTO"
---
TIPS
-Treats esphogeal varices
-Shunt to divert blood around liver
-Complication: worsening hepatic enceph (HE)
-improves esophageal varices and ascites

BRTO
-Treats gastric varices
-Embolize collaterals to drive flow into liver
-Complication: worsening esophageal varices and ascites
-Improves HE.

BRTO: access the portosystemic gastrorenal shunt from the left renal vein.
Balloon used to occlude the outlet of the gastrorenal or gastrocaval shunt.
Venogram then performed, and sclerosing agent used to treat the vessels.
Most common side effect: gross hematuria.
