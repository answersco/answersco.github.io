---
title: "Findings in osseous LCH:"
---
Eosinophilic granuloma (osseous LCH) &#8594; skeletal involvement only, and may be mono- or polyostotic.
May present with pain, tenderness, and fever (can mimic OM)
Usually 5-15 years of age
Skull: beveled edge lytic lesion
Flat bones (e.g. pelvis): hole within a pelvis lytic lesion
Long bone: permeative destruction, with later lytic lesion and faint rim of sclerosis
Spine: vertebra plana
Maxilla: floating teeth
