---
title: "Teaching considerations for the elderly"
---
1) assess for visual and hearing abilities (get their glasses or hearing aids if they need them)
2) speak clearly, facing client
3) use large print in bright colors for printed info (yellow paper with black font works best)
4) assist to develop easy dosing schedule
-use cues for remembering such as TV show, mealtime (has to be consistent)
-request daily dosing rather that several times/day
-instruct to not take OTC or herbals unless ask primary prescriber
5) use short time periods for teaching, 
6) review what was taught, teach-back method
7) use as many senses as possible: verbal, written, return demonstration to ensure learning
8) get family/caregiver involved as needed
