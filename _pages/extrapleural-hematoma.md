---
title: "Extrapleural hematoma"
---
Injury to chest wall that damages parietal pleura causes hemothorax
Injury to chest wall with intact parietal pleura- leads to extrapleural hematoma.
Classic history: persistent fluid collection after chest tube placement.
&quot;Displaced extrapleural fat&quot;

This is when you have a rib injury but the parietal pleura is NOT violated
Look for DISPLACEMENT of extrapleural fat 
Classic history is non-resolving collection after placement of chest tube
