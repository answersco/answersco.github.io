---
title: "Four CXR findings of PNA and associated etiology?"
---
1) Lobar consolidations: commonly bacterial or mycoplasma
2) Bulging fissures, pleural effusions, cavitation = bacterial.
3) Diffuse, fluffy infiltrate extending into the lung periphery: suggestive of bacterial infection
4) Central peribronchial opacities with atelectasis: suggestive of viral, Mycoplasma or Chlamydia infection.
