---
title: "Risk factors for infections"
---
1) environmental exposure: schools, prisons, especially during winter months w/ school closing windows
2) tissue destruction: skin is our best protection so when it is broken, it can cause a portal of entry
3) invasive procedure
4) malnutrition: do not have vitamins/minerals to keep them healthy
5) effects of pharmacological agents: antibiotics and chemo
6) age: newborns and elderly have decreased immune system
7) stress
8) heredity: genetic predisposed depending on how their immune system developed
9) existing disease: leukemia have damaged WBC, diabetes have poor circulation preventing antibodies and T cells to reach extremities
