A German monk who became one of the most famous critics of the Roman Catholic Church. In 1517, he wrote 95 theses, or statements of belief attacking the church practices. He led the Protestant Reformation.

German monk, priest, and theologian who led a church reform movement in the early 1500s by challenging &quot;indulgences&quot; and other practices within the Roman Catholic Church; his &quot;95 Theses&quot; sparked the Protestant Reformation; his central ideas included (1) the priesthood of all believers, (2) &quot;salvation by faith through grace&quot; as opposed to good works, (3) religious authority lay in the Holy Scripture alone -- all of which challenged the authority and control of the RCChurch
