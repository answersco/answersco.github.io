---
title: "Stereotactic biopsy"
---
Stereotactic biopsy&#8594; most commonly used to biopsy calcifications
Less commonly to biopsy a MM finding seen only on one view of a MM mass not seen on US
Contraindications:
Very thin breast (&lt; 3 cm compressed)
Far posterior or subareolar location
Inability to be positioned on stereotactic table
ASA and plavix are not contraindications
Uses targeting via paired stereo spot views and calculation of location in 3D space by computer.
Uses 11-gauge vacuum-assisted biopsy needle
Specimens must be radiographed to confirm that the targeted calcifications are in the sample.
