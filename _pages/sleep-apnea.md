---
title: "Sleep apnea"
---
Recurrent episodes of upper airway obstruction and a reduction in ventilation during sleep

-affects 18 million people in the US
-lack of airflow through the nose and mouth for periods of 10 seconds or longer during sleep
-includes obstructive apnea, central apnea, and mixed apnea
-hypopnea: shallow breathing
-hypoxia symptoms: cyanosis (late symptom), restlessness, anxiety, or change in mental status (first symptoms)

-individuals stop breathing while asleep
-snore mostly
-wake up when body stops breathing b/c the body is telling you to wake up which results in no deep sleep
