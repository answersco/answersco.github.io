---
title: "Miranda v. Arizona"
---
Supreme Court case under earl warren that declared that statements derived from the defendant during interrogation could only be applied when the defendant could consult with a lawyer and was informed that they did not need to self-incriminate themselves. Created Miranda Rights.

1966 landmark Supreme Court case in which the Court ruled that the 5th Amendment requires that individuals arrested for a crime must be advised of their right to remain silent and to have counsel present

(1966) The court ruled that those subjected to in-custody interrogation be advised of their constitutional right to an attorney and their right to remain silent.
