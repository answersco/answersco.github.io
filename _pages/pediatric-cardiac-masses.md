---
title: "Pediatric cardiac masses:"
---
Rhabdomyoma&#8594; most common cardiac tumor, primarily affecting babies &lt; 1 year with tuberous sclerosis.
Can cause arrhythmia or obstruction
Most common XR appearance is cardiomegaly, with variable pulmonary vasculature.
Teratoma&#8594; commonly attached to root of PA and aorta
May cause pleural effusion when arising from pericardium.
One of the primary causes of massive perinatal pericardial effusion.
Fibroma&#8594; most commonly in interventricular septum, causing arrhythmia and possibly outflow obstruction.
Cardiomegaly is most common imaging finding.
Hemangioma&#8594; also associated with massive pericardial effusion.
