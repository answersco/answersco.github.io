---
title: "What are the indications for TIPSS?"
---
Portal hypertension resulting in:
1) Acute variceal bleeding refractory to endoscopic management (Controls acute bleeding in 80-90%)
2) Variceal decompression to prevent recurrent bleeding (HVPG &gt; 12 mmHg)
3) Refractory ascites or hepatic hydrothorax
4) Budd-Chiari syndrome
5) Provide access for portal/mesenteric vein intervention (Thrombolysis, angioplasty, stenting, embolization)
6) Corrected sinusoidal pressure &gt; 12 mmHg
