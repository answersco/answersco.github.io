---
title: "What are the typical imaging appearances of autoimmune pancreatitis? Assoc lab findings? Tx?"
---
Sausage shaped pancreas with diffuse swelling, small rim of edema/pseudocapsule and loss of the normal fatty lobulations. Very increased IgG4 (sensitive and specific). Tx: Steroids.

CT: homogenous low attenuation masses with diffuse and intense postcontrast enhancement. 

MRI: high high T2, intense postcontrast enhancement, with flow voids

Dural-based supratentorial (90%) enhancing mass &#8594; cortical buckling, trapped CSF/vessels in &quot;cleft&quot; between tumor and brain. 25% calcify.
