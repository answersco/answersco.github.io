---
title: "Solitary hypodense parenchymal mass on NECT"
---
Trauma, ischemia, infection, demyelinating disease, cerebritis, encephalitis, neoplasm, etc. A parenchymal hematoma loses about 1.5 HU in density every day. Typically, subacute hematomas (between a few days and a week old) appear mostly isodense (not hypodense) relative to brain.
