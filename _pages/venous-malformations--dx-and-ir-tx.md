---
title: "Venous malformations: Dx and IR tx"
---
VM: usually isolated and sporadic, but can be associated with syndromes such as KTS
-Soft, nonpulsatile, and decompress when patient is prone.
-Superficial lesions can present with bleeding, limb discoloration, local thrombosis, and pain with ambulation. Deep lesions may only present as limb swelling.
-Angio: normal arteries with faint or absent opacification of the lesion. Venography (esp with direct puncture) allows dx.
-Sclerosis with alcohol, sodium tetradecyl sulfate, or polidocanol is effective.
-GA usually necessary
-Commonly multiple treatments needed.
