---
title: "What is biliary atresia? Imaging findings? Assoc? Tx? Ddx?"
---
Absent or severely deficient extrahepatic biliary tree. Irregular or absent gallbladder. No excretion of biliary agent (MRCP or choletec). If nuc scan pretreat with phenobarb to enhance liver. Progressive conjugated (direct) hyperbilirubinemia in neonatal period. Tx: Kasai portoenterostomy is 90% effective if performed before 2 months of age. Ddx: hepatitis, bile plug (CF), choledochal cyst.
