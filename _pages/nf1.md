---
NF1/von Recklinghausen disease &#8594; prominent skin manifestations (e.g. cafe au lait spots), peripheral nerve sheath tumors, CNS malignancies, and bone abnormalities
50% AD, 50% sporadic (chromosome 17)
Bright spots on T2-weighted images &#8594; seen most commonly in children aged 4-12, and become less prominent in adults.
Target sign: central T2-hypointensity seen in schwannomas and neurofibromas, reflecting a benign entity.
Unlike schwannomas, neurofibromas are not encapsulated and involve entire cross-sectional area of the nerve.
Lisch nodules: hamartomas of iris
