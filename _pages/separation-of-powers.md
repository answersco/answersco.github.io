---
title: "Separation of Powers"
---
Constitutional division of powers among the legislative. Executive, and judicial branches. Legislative makes the law, executive applies/enforces the law, and the judiciary interpreting the law.

a way of dividing the power of government among the legislative, executive, and judicial branches, each staffed separately, with equality and independence of each branch ensured by the Constitution

refers to the division of power among the legislative, judicial, and executive branches of government

There is no &quot;head&quot; of the government and all of the powers are separated.
