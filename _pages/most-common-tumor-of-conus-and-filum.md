---
title: "Most common tumor of conus and filum:"
---
Myxopapillary ependymoma&#8594; ependymoma variant that occurs exclusively within the conus medullaris or filum terminale.
Most common tumor of conus and filum.
Characterized by slow growth&#8594; classically leading to vertebral scalloping and spinal canal enlargement.
Highly vascular, hemorrhagic tumor, with complex, lobulated MRI appearance with internal hemorrhage.
Peripheral hemosiderin often present &#8594; T2-hypointensity and blooming on GRE.
