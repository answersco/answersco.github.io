---
title: "Legg-Calve-Perthes (LCP):"
---
Legg-Calve-Perthes (LCP) &#8594; AVN of the capital femoral epiphysio-ossification center, usually in children 4-8 years.
Usually unilateral: bilateral LCP may reflect sickle cell disease or steroid therapy.
Early LCP: may show subtle sclerosis of the femoral head on XR
Typically &#8595; uptake on bone scan.
Late LCP: shows secondary signs of osteonecrosis, with flattened and distorted femoral head and secondary osteoarthritis.
Bone scan typically shows &#8593; uptake due to attempted repair
