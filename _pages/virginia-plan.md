---
title: "Virginia Plan"
---
Fourteen proposals by the Virginia Delegation to the Constitutional Convention for creating a more powerful central government. Driven by James Madison, it introduced the legislative, executive and judicial branches to balance power. Gave states proportional representation. Adopted by Big states

plan liked by the large states because representation was based only on population

the first general plan for the Constitution offered in Philadelphia; its key points were a bicameral legislature, and an executive and a judiciary by the national legislature
