---
title: "Pulmonary angiography: contraindications and classic catheter"
---
Pulmonary HTN with elevated right heart pressures (&gt;70 systolic and 20 end-diastolic)
-If you need to proceed anyway, use low-osmolar contrast agents injected into the right or left PA (not the main PA)

LBBB- catheter in right heart can cause right heart block 
(and thus complete block)
-If you need to proceed anyway, perform prophylactic pacing.

Grollman catheter: preshaped 7Fr catheter- classic tool for pulmonary angio
