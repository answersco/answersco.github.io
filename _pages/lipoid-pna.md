---
title: "Lipoid PNA"
---
Exogenous: commonly old people who drink/aspirate mineral oil (as a laxative).
XR: area of lung opacification that is chronic, or slowly increases with time.
CT: low attenuation/fat density in the consolidation
May also appear as crazy paving.

Acute exogenous lipoid PNA: children who accidentally ingest hydrocarbons.

Endogenous: more common
Secondary to post-obstructive processes (e.g. cancer) and buildup of lipid-laden macrophages.
