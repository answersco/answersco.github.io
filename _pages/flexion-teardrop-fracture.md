---
&#8226; Most severe/devastating flexion injury
&#8226; Usually lower cervical spine C5-6 (70% of cases)
&#8226; Diving accident shallow pool common cause
&#8226; Immediate, complete and permanent quadriplegia (90% of cases)
&#8226; *Acute anterior cord syndrome* - loss pain, temperature, and touch retention position, motion, vibration (posterior column senses)

unstable flexion fracture
Shearing off the entire anterior aspect of the vertebral body

Most severe C-spine fracture?

Disruption of posterior C-spine ligaments with anterior vertebral body compression. Usually associated with spinal cord injury.
