---
title: "Social control theory"
---
theory developed by Hirschi that states that drug use/criminal behavior are more likely to occur in the absence of social controls to conform (attachment to family/peers, commitment to/belief in conformity)

theory that emphasizes the degree to which strong bonds with family, school, work, religion, and other social institutions motivate individuals to engage in responsible behavior and refrain from substance use

theory that assumes that deviant behavior is attractive and we need to explain what controls or constrains our behavior
