---
title: "Typical age and location for unicameral bone cyst, giant cell tumor, and eosinophilic granuloma"
---
Unicameral bone cyst: usually diagnosed in 5-10 year old patients, most commonly in the proximal humeral and proximal femoral metaphyses.
-Usually not symptomatic unless pathologic fracture.

GCT: usually in EPIPHYSIS
-More common in 3rd and 4th decade when growth plates are closed.

EG: can be located in epiphysis and metaphysis and occur mainly in adolescents and young adults.
