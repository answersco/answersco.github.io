---
title: "Parsonage-Turner syndrome"
---
Idiopathic denervation of the brachial plexus

Idiopathic brachial neuropathy. May cause atrophy of rotator cuff. Diagnosis of exlcusion. Must rule out structural cause of neuropahty

Fatty atrophy in distributions of multiple nerves (suprascapular nerve and axillary nerve)

(neurogenic amyotrophy) severe acute pain in the shoulder radiating into the arm, neck and back.

Neurogenic muscle edema corresponding to a particular nerve. Suprascapular nerve: Supraspinatus, Infraspinatus. Axillary nerve: Teres minor, Deltoid. Acute pain followed by weakness. Associations: Vaccinations, Viral illness, General anesthesia.
