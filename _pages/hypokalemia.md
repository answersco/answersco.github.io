---
title: "Hypokalemia"
---
-pt. who have low potassium
-can have cramping (charlie horses)
-foods high in K+ are bananas, potato skins, oranges
-give water and then potassium
-if you don't have enough you will see ekg/rhythm changes
-replacing potassium by giving pills
-call physician if pt. potassium level is below 3.0
-potassium can be given iv but it always has to be diluted (on a piggyback) they would flat line if you give concentrated potassium
