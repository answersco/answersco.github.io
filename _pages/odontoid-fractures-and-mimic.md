---
title: "Odontoid fractures and mimic"
---
Type 1: Upper part of odontoid (rare)&#8594; may be stable
Type 2: fracture at base &#8594; unstable
Type 3: fracture through dens into body of C2 &#8594; unstable, but best prognosis for healing

Os Odontoideum&#8594; can mimic type 1 fracture
Prone to subluxation and instability
Associated with Morquio's syndrome
Orthotopic: positioned on top of the dens
Dystopic: fused to the clivus
