---
title: "What are findings of papillomas?"
---
Papilloma = most common intraductal mass and cause of bloody discharge; women ~50 yo in subareolar region 1 cm from nipple
- often normal mammogram, possible calcs
- occurs within a main duct/lactiferous sinus
- US = well def smooth walled hypoechoic mass, possibly asociated ductal dilatation = LOOK FOR VASCULAR stalk
- Galactography = solitary filling defect with dilated duct
- Multiple papillllomas = tend to be more peripheral
