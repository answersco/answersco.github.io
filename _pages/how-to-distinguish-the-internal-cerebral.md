---
title: "How to distinguish the internal cerebral veins from the inferior sagittal sinus and veins of Labbe?"
---
Internal cerebral veins are located &quot;between&quot; (superior to Labbe and inferior to inferior sag sinus) and are SINUSOIDAL in appearance
- they drain into Great vein of Galen --&gt; straight sinus
- on axial CT, they are located within the triangle of the velum interpositum
