---
title: "What is the appearance of hyperacute blood on CT?"
---
Hyperacute &quot;unclotted&quot; hemorrhage ( &lt; 2-3 hours old) is hypodense or isodense depending on cell and protein count and appears similarly to very chronic hemorrhagic collections ( &gt; 10-14 days old), and nonhemorrhagic collections (e.g., subdural effusions or hygromas)
