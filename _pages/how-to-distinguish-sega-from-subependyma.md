---
title: "How to distinguish SEGA from subependymal nodule in a tuberous sclerosis patient?"
---
SIZE (&lt; 1 cm is likely subependymal nodule)

- SEGA usually presents 2-3 cm with obstructive hydrocephalus

* Both can be calcified, enhance, and occur in adolescent Tuberous Sclerosis patients (&lt; 20 yo)
