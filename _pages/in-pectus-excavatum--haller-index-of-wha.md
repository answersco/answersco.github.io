---
title: "In pectus excavatum, Haller index of what will be considered for surgical correction?"
---
Index &gt; 3.25

(Divide the TRANSVERSE (bigger diameter) by the AP smaller diameter)

Because the depressed sternum abuts the right atrium, patients can easily be misdiagnosed as having right middle lobe collapse or pneumonia because of apparent effacement of the right heart border on the frontal radiograph. However, the lateral projection is usually sufficient to identify the cause.
