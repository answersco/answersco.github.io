---
title: "What symptoms are more commonly associated with ZMC and LeFort fractures?"
---
ZMC fracture: more commonly features restricted ocular motion, cheek hypesthesia (due to infraorbital nerve injury), and depression of the malar eminence.

LeFort fractures: more commonly involve midface retrusion, mobility of the midface, and dental malocclusion.

Some orbital and skull base injuries: may result in optic nerve compromise, enophthalmos, and CSF rhinorrhea.
