---
title: "Malignant splenic tumors"
---
Splenic angiosarcoma: rare, aggressive, poor prognosis, associated with thorotrast
CT: may manifest as a poorly-defined area of heterogeneity or low density in an enlarged spleen.
May contain necrosis.
Can become large enough to rupture.
Usually poor enhancement

Lymphoma: most common malignant tumor of spleen
Splenomegaly is most common finding
High grade lymphomas and HL may show discrete nodules of tumor
Low-density on CT, T1 dark, PET-HOT

Mets: most commonly melanoma, followed by breast and lung.
