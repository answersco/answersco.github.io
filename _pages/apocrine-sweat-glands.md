---
title: "Apocrine sweat glands"
---
-Locations: groin, anal region, axilla, areola, beard area in men
-Inactive until puberty
-Ducts lead to nearby hair follicles
-Produce sweat that is milky and contains fatty acids
-Respond to stress and sexual stimulation
-Believed to secrete pheromones&#8212;chemicals that can influence behavior of others
-Bromhidrosis&#8212;disagreeable body odor produced by bacterial action on sweat from apocrine glands
