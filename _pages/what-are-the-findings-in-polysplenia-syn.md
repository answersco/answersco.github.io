---
title: "What are the findings in polysplenia syndrome?"
---
Polysplenia syndrome = double left-sidedness (left sided isomerism)
Multiple spleens, if stomach on R look for spleens posterior to stomach.
Azygous continuation of IVC (&gt; 70%), hepatic veins drain separately into common atrium
Bilateral SVC (41%), 1 or both may connect to coronary sinus
Left isomerism of atrial appendages
Common atrium or large ostium primum ASD
Bilateral bi-lobed lungs, Bilateral hyparterial bronchi
