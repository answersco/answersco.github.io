---
title: "Normal anatomy of left pulmonary arteries"
---
At the hilum the left PA gives off its first branches to the upper lobe- usually multiple individual or paired arteries.
A single common upper lobe trunk seen in &lt; 20%
Lingula usually supplied next from descending portion of LPA before it divides into the lower lobe vessels.
Like the RPA, the lower lobe LPA branches approximate the segmental bronchial anatomy, but are highly variable
