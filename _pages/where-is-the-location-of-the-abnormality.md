---
title: "Where is the location of the abnormality in Mondor disease?"
---
Answer: the dermal junction
Mondor disease: thrombophlebitis of a superficial vein (usually thoracoepigastric or lateral thoracic vein) in the breast
US: anechoic or hypoechoic tubular structure with a beaded appearance, located at the dermal junction.
MM is often normal
Typically no treatment other than anti-inflammatory medication as needed.
