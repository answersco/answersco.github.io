---
title: "Fibrous dysplasia features?"
---
No periostitis, unless fracture. May be monostotic or polyostotic. Predilection for pelvis, proximal femur, ribs (lytic posterior ribs, sclerotic anterior ribs), skull. When in pelvis proximal femur involved. 
McCune-Albright syndrome: Polyostotic fibrous dysplasia, cafe-au-lait spots, precocious puberty, girl. Adamantinoma: Tibial lesion that resembles fibrous dysplasia (RARE!). 
Cherubism: Multiple fibrous dysplasia lesions in the jaw.
Mazabraud: polyostotic FD, middle aged woman, soft tissue myxomas, risk of osseious malignant transformation
