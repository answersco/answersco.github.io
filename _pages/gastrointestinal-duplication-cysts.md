---
title: "Gastrointestinal Duplication Cysts"
---
Cystic lesion, most often abutting GI tract, with well-defined wall
Bowel wall signature on ultrasound: Echogenic mucosa, hypoechoic muscular layer, echogenic serosa
Can occur anywhere along alimentary tract
75% intraabdominal
20% thoracic
5% thoracoabdominal
Most often associated with jejunum/ileum (53%) and esophagus (18%)
Ultrasound is best for intraabdominal lesions
CT or MR better for intrathoracic lesions
Consider survey of remaining chest or abdomen to exclude synchronous lesions, seen in 10-20%
