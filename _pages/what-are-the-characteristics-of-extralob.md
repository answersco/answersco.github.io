---
title: "What are the characteristics of extralobar pulm sequest?"
---
Only 25% of all. Separate from normal lung and has own pleural layers. Systemic arterial supply. Most drain to the IVC or azygous creating a L to R shunt. More common in males. Usually located in the L CPA. Other locations include mediastinum, pericardium or retroperitoneum. Also included in Ddx of adrenal mass in neonate. Tissue may be aerated with small cysts. May also maintain a connection to the GI tract.
