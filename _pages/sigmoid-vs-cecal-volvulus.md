---
title: "Sigmoid vs cecal volvulus"
---
Sigmoid volvulus: overall more common, and typically older patients (chronic constipation is predisposing factor)
Buzzwords: &quot;coffee-bean sign&quot; or &quot;inverted 3 sign&quot;
Typically points to RUQ
50% recur after decompression

Cecal volvulus: younger person, associated with &quot;long mesentery.&quot;
More commonly points to LUQ.
Overall much less common.

Cecal bascule: anterior folding of cecum without twisting.
Dilated cecum in ectopic position in middle abdomen, without a mesenteric twist.
