---
title: "REM sleep"
---
-20% to 25% of a person's nightly sleep time
-vivid, colorful, implausible dreams
-increase in length with each pass through sleep cycle so you have more REM sleep the closer you are to waking up -- go through several REM cycles at night so not all dreams are remembered
-decrease in muscle tone except in eyes and facial muscles
-increase in cerebral blood flow and O2 consumption
-fluctuation or increase BP, pulse, respirations
-pulse, respiratory rate, blood pressure, metabolic rate, and body temperature increase; skeletal muscle tone and deep tendon reflexes are depressed
-important to emotional and mental equilibrium, learning, memory and adaptation
