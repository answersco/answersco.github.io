---
title: "OA in the hand:"
---
In order of decreasing involvement, typical sites of OA in hand include:
DIPs &#8594; most common site of OA in hands is 2nd DIP
Base of thumb at the first CMC
PIPs
Unlike RA, the MCPs are less commonly affected
Large osteophytes&#8594; cause characteristic soft-tissue swelling surrounding the finger joints.
Heberden node&#8594; swelling around the DIP
Bouchard node&#8594; around the PIP
