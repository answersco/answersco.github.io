---
title: "What are the imaging findings CT and MRI of DNET?"
---
Location: 60% in temporal lobes; 30% frontal
CT: well-demarcated, wedge-shaped lobulated hypodense cortical mass, often scallops inner table of calvarium (40-60%)
MRI: Peripheral triangular shaped lesion with minimal to no mass effect or peritumoral edema. Slow growth. T1 hypo, T2 hyperintense with bubbly appearance. Diffusion is similar to CSF. Usually no enhancement or calcifications.
