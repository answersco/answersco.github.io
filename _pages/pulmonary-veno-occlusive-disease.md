title: "Pulmonary veno-occlusive disease?"
---
Fibrotic obliteration of the pulmonary veins and venules.

Pulmonary veno-occlusive disease (WHO group 1, post-capillary)
PAH secondary to pulmonary veno-occlusive disease is caused by fibrotic obliteration of the pulmonary veins and venules&#8594; may be idiopathic or associated with pregnancy, drugs (especially bleomycin), and BMT.
Imaging features PA enlargement, commonly with pulmonary edema and ground-glass centrilobular nodules.

Pulmonary arterial hypertension affecting post-capillary pulmonary vasculature = Normal Wedge pressure!
- Occlusion of pulmonary veins by fibrous tissue in a patchy distribution
