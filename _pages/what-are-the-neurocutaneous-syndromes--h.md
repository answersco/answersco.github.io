---
title: "What are the neurocutaneous syndromes? How are they differentiated?"
---
Tuberous sclerosis (TS): Multiple hamartomas, brain, renal, lung.
Sturge-Weber: Retinal, trigeminal, and pial angiomatosis: port wine stain, brain atrophy and calcs.
NF 1: VonRecklinghausen. optic nerve gliomas and plexiform neurofibromas.
Neurofibromatosis type 2 (NF2): Multiple meningiomas and schwannomas.
PHACES: Posterior fossa malformations, hemangiomas, arterial anomalies, cardiac anomalies, eye anomalies, and sternal clefting
von Hippel-Lindau disease (VHL): Hemangioblastomas, retinal angiomas, renal AMLs/RCC, Endolymphatic sac tumor.
