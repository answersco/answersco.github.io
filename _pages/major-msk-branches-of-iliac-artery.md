---
title: "Major MSK branches of iliac artery"
---
Obturator and inferior gluteal- both commonly give branches to bladder

Obturator: 50% from anterior division of IIA, 15% from inferior gluteal or internal pudendal, 20% from superior gluteal (posterior division).
Exits pelvis through obturator canal, where it has a characteristic bifurcation.

Inferior gluteal: branch of anterior division in 75%, posterior in 25%.
-Exits pelvis between piriformis and coccygeus muscles in lower portion of sciatic notch. 
-Accompanies the sciatic and posterior femoral cutaneous nerves
