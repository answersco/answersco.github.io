starting with agricultural overproduction following WWI and exacerbated by the collapse of the US stock market in 1929, this period saw worldwide economic stagnation; heavy borrowing by European nations from USA during WWI further contributed to instability in European economies and sharp declines in income and production as buying and selling slowed down resulting in widespread unemployment; countries raised tariffs to protect their industries furthering the stagnation of trade and American withdrawal of European investments
