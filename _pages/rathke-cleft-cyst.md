---
title: "Rathke cleft cyst"
---
Arise from squamous epithelial remnants of anterior lobe of pituitary gland. Usually asymptomatic. Cyst may contain mucus, serous fluid, or cellular debris. Variable signal intensities. May mimic craniopharyngioma.

Cyst in between the ANTERIOR and POSTERIOR pituitary glands
If you see a central DOT this is pathognomonic for a rathke cleft cyst (seen 75% of the time)
T2 bright T1 variable

-T1 bright
-NO ENHANCEMENT

usu incidental and very bright on T2, NO enhancement
