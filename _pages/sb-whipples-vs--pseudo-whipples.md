---
title: "SB Whipples vs. Pseudo-Whipples"
---
Whipples: usually males in 50s
Marked swelling of intestinal villi and thickened, irregular mucosal folds, primarily in duodenum and proximal jejunum.
Buzzwords = &quot;Sand-like nodules,&quot; referring to diffuse micronodules in the jejunum. 
Jejunal mucosal folds are thickened.
Low-density, enlarged lymph nodes.

Pseudo-Whipples: MAI infection in AIDS patients with CD4 &lt; 100.
Nodules in jejunum, like Whipples.
Also have splenomegaly and large retroperitoneal nodes.
