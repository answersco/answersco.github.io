---
title: "Key performance indicators"
---
Key Performance Indicators (KPI): financial and nonfinancial measures that are used to define and evaluate the success of an organization.
Devised to help evaluate the progress of an organization toward achieving its long-term goals and fulfilling its vision.
Should be amenable to reproducible measurement and remeasurement.

In healthcare organizations, performance assessment is critical for the development of best practices that can lead to improved outcomes in patient care.
KPIs for radiology are non universal; each institution must develop its own.
Typically include measures of patient safety, quality of care, customer service, appropriateness of imaging utilization, and measures of productivity and financial performance.

Quantifiable metrics a company uses to evaluate progress toward critical success factors
These can be FINANCIAL and NON-FINANCIAL
